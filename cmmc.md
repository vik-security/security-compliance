## Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC)

Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC), evaluates organization's security posture as it relates to the handling of Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI). CMMC L2 is soon to become a mandatory requirement, in Q4 2026, for contractors operating within the defense space who work with CUI. Organizations pursuing CMMC certification are typically advised to work with qualified assessors to help validate that required security controls are implemented and operating effectively. Because of its strong emphasis on access control, monitoring and data protection, CMMC is often part of discussions around strong security programs, identity security and Zero Trust architectures.

### CMMC three maturity levels:
* Level 1 – Foundational cybersecurity practices
* Level 2 – Advanced protection of Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)
* Level 3 – Expert-level security practices for highly sensitive defense programs
More information can be found through the U.S. Department of Defense:
https://dodcio.defense.gov/cmmc/About/

## Relationship to NIST SP 800-171
 

NIST SP 800-171 is the primary security standard used to protect Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) in non-federal systems and organizations. The controls required for CMMC Level 2 are derived from the security requirements outlined in NIST SP 800-171.
Organizations pursuing CMMC Level 3 must implement additional security requirements from NIST SP 800-172, which introduces enhanced protections designed to defend against more advanced threat actors.

NIST SP 800-171 defines 14 control families containing 110 security requirements, while NIST SP 800-172 introduces 35 enhanced security requirements focused on areas such as penetration-resistant architecture, damage-limiting operations and cyber resiliency. Due to the depth of these controls and the operational maturity required to implement them effectively, achieving CMMC compliance can be a complex process depending on the organization's infrastructure and security maturity.

## Outline of SP 800-171 14 control families 

* Access Control (AC) – System access is restircted to authorized users and devices only.
* Awareness and Training (AT) – Personnel receive security awareness training in accordance to their role.
* Audit and Accountability (AU) – Audit logs are reviewed to monitor system activity.
* Configuration Management (CM) – Secure system configurations and set up and maintianed.
* Identification and Authentication (IA) – Identities are verified before granting system access.
* Incident Response (IR) – Detection, response, analysis, containment, recovery, post incident reviews.
* Maintenance (MA) – System maintenance activities are monitored and montrolled.
* Media Protection (MP) – Physical and digital media is managed and protected by policies.
* Personnel Security (PS) – Personnel with system access are properly and consistently authorized.
* Physical Protection (PE) – Facilities and physical systems are protected from unauthorized access.
* Risk Assessment (RA) – Risks to organizational systems and data are evaluated and mitigated.
* Security Assessment (CA) – Security controls are consistently reviewed.
* System and Communications Protection (SC) – System communications and data in transit are protected.
* System and Information Integrity (SI) – Systems all monitored for vulnerabilities, malware and unauthorized changes.

For detailed description, please see:

* NIST SP 800-172 - https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/sp/800/171/r2/upd1/final
